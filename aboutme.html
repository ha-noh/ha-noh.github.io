10/16/20

It feels strange to scrap something I've invested so much into, but it's come time to say goodbye to the lightmode toggle :(.

Night mode's purpose - as in most implementations - was to provide an alternate, less eye straining, display method. The website backgrounds were white by default to support the comic book aesthetic, and the night mode was there because who actually likes light mode. So really, from the start having a toggle was a compromise between aesthetics and comfort.

Having two modes for every page brought twice the issues: Every font color had to display nicely on both background colors; the blinding flash of white as the page rendered led to night mode being toggled by default; adding colors to the palette was more labor-intensive than it should have been; every page had to have the nav element containing the toggle button. This element even lost most its purpose over time, and eventually only existed to hold that one button.

The feature is more prohibitive than useful, and it's time for it to be scrapped. While it was fun to think about, unfortunately it's no longer needed.
